UNIGO methodology

Impact Scoring Framework

A clear beta-stage system for assessing businesses without inventing impact claims.

Beta principle

UNIGO uses only public evidence found on a business's own website. Scores are provisional, not audited, and may change when businesses submit additional documentation.

CRS

Carbon Reduction Score

Energy, waste, mobility, circularity, sourcing and resource efficiency.

GES

Gender Equality Score

Women-led ownership, leadership, pay commitments and documented equality practices.

LGBTQIS

LGBTQ+ Inclusion Score

Inclusion policies, partnerships, training, safe-space commitments and community support.

LES

Local Economy Score

Local sourcing, local employment, independent ownership and local value creation.

CIS

Community Impact Score

Education, volunteering, cultural exchange, accessibility and community programmes.

Scoring scale

Each category is worth 20 points.

0 pointsNo public evidence5 pointsGeneral statement or commitment10 pointsSpecific documented practice15 pointsStrong, repeated practice20 pointsMeasured result, certification or independent evidence

Evidence status

Transparency before verification.

StatusMeaning
Public website evidenceUNIGO reviewed information published by the business.
Evidence submittedThe business supplied additional documentation for review.

Important safeguards

UNIGO does not estimate CO₂ savings, local economic value, beneficiaries or other quantitative outcomes unless the business publishes the figure with a credible source or methodology. Missing evidence is not treated as proof of poor impact.

Traveler pilot

Impact Credits remain separate.

Business Impact Scores assess business practices. Traveler Impact Credits reward verified actions such as participating, purchasing locally, volunteering or organising. The beta will first showcase this as a simple demonstration before introducing live individual scoring.
