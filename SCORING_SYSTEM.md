# UNIGO Launch Scoring System

This file is the internal source of truth for the initial launch.

## Shared dimensions

Both businesses and travelers use five dimensions, each capped at 20 points:

- CRS — Carbon Reduction
- GES — Gender Equality
- LGBTQIS — LGBTQ+ Inclusion
- LES — Local Economy
- CIS — Community Impact

Maximum total score: 100.

## Business Impact Score (BIS)

Business scores measure available evidence, not assumed impact.

For each dimension:

- 0 — no evidence available
- 5 — public commitment or general statement
- 10 — specific documented practice
- 15 — strong or repeated practice
- 20 — measured result, certification, or independent evidence

BIS = CRS + GES + LGBTQIS + LES + CIS.

Launch tiers:

- Emerging: 0–39
- Committed: 40–59
- Impact Partner: 60–79
- Impact Leader: 80–100

Launch rule: all directory scores are provisional until reviewed with the business.

## Traveler Impact Score

Traveler scores measure verified actions.

Action values:

- Participate: +2 — visit, purchase, attendance, or short approved activity
- Contribute: +5 — volunteering or meaningful participation
- Lead: +10 — organizing, hosting, mentoring, or leading an approved action

Each approved action is assigned to one primary dimension before it is published. The score in each dimension is capped at 20.

Traveler Impact Score = CRS + GES + LGBTQIS + LES + CIS.

Traveler levels:

- Explorer: 0–24
- Connector: 25–49
- Changemaker: 50–74
- Ambassador: 75–100

Launch verification:

- partner confirmation, QR check-in, receipt, ticket, or participation record
- the same action cannot be counted twice

## Launch badges

Badges do not add score. Initial badges:

- First Impact — 1 verified action
- Local Supporter — 5 partner visits
- City Explorer — actions in 3 cities
- Consistent Traveler — activity in 3 different months

## Product principle

Keep the public explanation brief. Add more advanced progression rules, badge families, weighting, expiration, or anti-gaming controls only after launch data shows they are needed.
