Frequently asked questions

UNIGO FAQ

Clear answers about the beta, scoring, pilot businesses and the Impact Passport.

About UNIGO

What is UNIGO?

UNIGO is an Impact Travel Platform that helps travelers discover meaningful experiences, support ethical businesses and build a personal record of positive actions through an Impact Passport.

What does “Travel Beyond Tourism” mean?

It means treating travel as more than consumption. UNIGO encourages travelers to support local communities, inclusive initiatives, social enterprises and lower-impact experiences.

Where is the pilot focused?

The first business directory focuses mainly on Utrecht, followed by Amsterdam and Rotterdam.

Beta access

What is the UNIGO beta?

The beta is an early public version used to test demand, usability, business participation and the scoring concept before building the full platform.

Is joining the beta free?

Yes. Registering interest in the beta is free.

What can beta users do?

Beta users can explore pilot businesses and experiences, review the scoring model, see a demo Impact Passport and provide feedback.

Business scoring

What is the Business Impact Score?

It is a provisional score from 0 to 100 based on five categories: Carbon Reduction, Gender Equality, LGBTQ+ Inclusion, Local Economy and Community Impact.

Are the listed businesses verified by UNIGO?

Not yet. Current scores are based only on evidence published on the organization’s own website. They are clearly marked as provisional.

What happens when there is no public evidence?

UNIGO assigns zero points for that criterion rather than making assumptions. This does not mean the business has no positive practice; it means the evidence was not publicly available.

Can a business correct or improve its profile?

Yes. Businesses can claim their profile, submit documents, correct missing information and apply for a future UNIGO Verified score.

Impact Passport and Credits

What is the Impact Passport?

The Impact Passport is a personal record of verified actions, Impact Credits, impact dimensions and earned badges.

Are the current passport numbers real?

No. The current passport is an illustrative beta example designed to show how the system could work.

How are Impact Credits earned?

Credits can be linked to awareness, participation and leadership actions. Examples include attending a verified event, supporting a local business, volunteering or organizing a meaningful initiative.

Are traveler credits the same as business scores?

No. Traveler Impact Credits reward actions. Business Impact Scores assess publicly documented business practices.

Trust and methodology

Does UNIGO estimate CO₂ savings or local economic value?

Not without a defensible methodology or a credible published source. UNIGO avoids inventing quantified impact claims.

How will verification work later?

The next stage will allow businesses and facilitators to submit supporting evidence for review against the published framework.
