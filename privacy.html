Privacy Policy

Last updated: 30 June 2026.

UNIGO respects privacy and collects only the information needed to operate the website, communicate with people who choose to engage with us, review organization applications, and improve the platform responsibly.

Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to visitors, traveler sign-ups, organization applicants, community contacts, and people who communicate with UNIGO through the website or direct outreach.

Information we may collect

  • Name, email address, city or country, organization name, and message content submitted through forms.
  • Organization information provided by applicants or available from public sources.
  • Technical information such as browser, device, pages visited, and approximate usage information where analytics is accepted.
  • Communication records when you contact UNIGO or respond to outreach.

Why we use this information

  • To respond to requests, applications, and messages.
  • To manage traveler and partner interest during launch.
  • To review organization profiles and correction requests.
  • To improve website usability, security, and content.
  • To comply with legal obligations and protect legitimate interests.

Legal bases

Where GDPR applies, UNIGO relies on one or more legal bases: consent, performance of requested pre-contractual steps, legitimate interests, and legal obligation. Optional analytics is used only with consent. Direct communications and organization review may rely on legitimate interests where appropriate and balanced with individual rights.

Analytics and cookies

UNIGO does not require optional analytics for access to the website. Google Analytics should only load after consent. See the Cookie Policy for more information.

Sharing information

UNIGO does not sell personal data. Information may be shared only with service providers needed to operate the website, hosting, database, analytics after consent, email or administration, or where required by law.

International transfers

Some technology providers may process data outside the European Economic Area. Where this occurs, UNIGO aims to rely on appropriate safeguards such as standard contractual clauses or equivalent mechanisms provided by the service provider.

Retention

UNIGO keeps personal information only as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose collected, unless a longer period is required by law. Launch sign-up and application data should be reviewed periodically and deleted or anonymized when no longer needed.

Your rights

Depending on your location and circumstances, you may have the right to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, portability, or withdrawal of consent. You may also object to direct communications at any time.

Security

UNIGO uses reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect information. No online system can be guaranteed completely secure, but we aim to reduce risk through minimization, access control, secure hosting, and careful data handling.

Children

UNIGO is not intended for children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Complaints

If you have concerns, contact UNIGO first so we can respond. You may also have the right to contact your local data protection authority, including the Dutch Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens where applicable.

Contact

Privacy questions, deletion requests, or correction requests can be sent to privacy@unigo.now.
